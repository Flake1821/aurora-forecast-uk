About UK Aurora & Night Sky

UK Aurora & Night Sky provides night-sky alerts for the whole of the UK, covering aurora activity, meteor showers, eclipses, supermoons, planetary events and rare atmospheric phenomena, alongside occasional astrophotography images and tips.

We monitor space weather data from NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center, GFZ Potsdam's half-hourly Hp30 index, and AuroraWatch UK to bring you timely alerts when there's a chance of seeing the aurora borealis or other night sky events from anywhere in the UK.

Aurora visibility depends on your latitude. Southern England (around 50°N) typically needs Kp 5+, while Scotland (around 56°N) may see aurora at Kp 3. Set your location for personalised Kp thresholds and local weather.

How to Read the Dashboard

The dashboard is designed to be understood at a glance, with no scientific jargon. All labels and descriptions use plain English so you can quickly decide whether it's worth heading outside. Everything updates automatically every 5 minutes.

UK Aurora Activity Now

This panel shows the live AuroraWatch UK status from Lancaster University's magnetometer network. The colour badge (Green / Yellow / Amber / Red) tells you how disturbed the UK's magnetic field is right now. Green means no alert; Red means a strong geomagnetic storm is in progress and aurora may be visible across much of the UK.

Below the badge, your local aurora chance shows whether aurora is likely from your location based on the current activity level and your latitude threshold. A summary line explains the overall situation, including cloud and daylight conditions.

Gauges

The two gauges show real-time geomagnetic activity. Both are colour-coded from green (quiet) through yellow and orange to red (storm). The text below each gauge changes colour to match the dial, so you can read the meaning without checking the number.

  • Global Magnetic Activity (Hp30) — updated every 30 minutes from GFZ Potsdam. This is the most up-to-date measure of worldwide geomagnetic activity. Higher values mean stronger disturbance.
  • Storm Strength Forecast (Kp) — the 3-hourly forecast from NOAA. This predicts how strong the next geomagnetic period will be. When it reaches your location's threshold, aurora becomes possible.
Aurora Tonight

A quick summary of tonight's viewing conditions: the peak forecast Kp, current Hp30, dark hours, cloud outlook, and moon phase. The badge (None / Poor / Fair / Good / Excellent) gives you an overall rating. If aurora is possible but cloud is heavy, it will tell you.

Weather & Conditions

The Right Now panel shows current weather at your location: cloud cover percentage, temperature, wind, and visibility. The Cloud Forecast bar chart shows hour-by-hour cloud cover for the next 12 hours so you can spot any clear gaps. The Moon panel shows tonight's phase, rise/set times, and whether moonlight will interfere with faint aurora. The Light Pollution panel shows your Bortle scale rating — lower numbers mean darker skies and better chances of seeing faint aurora.

Should I Go Outside?

This is the single most important panel. It combines all the factors — aurora activity, cloud cover, darkness, moonlight, and magnetic conditions — into one clear recommendation:

  • Yes! — Conditions are good. Head outside to a dark spot.
  • Worth a look — Conditions are mixed. Check the northern horizon.
  • Marginal — Conditions could improve. Keep monitoring.
  • Not tonight — One or more factors are blocking the view.

The two bullet points below the verdict show the most important reasons driving the recommendation, in priority order. If there is a good viewing window in the next 12 hours, it will be shown with the expected cloud cover.

Solar Wind, IMF Bz & Storm Scale

These three panels show the real-time conditions in space that drive aurora:

  • Solar Wind — speed and density of particles flowing from the Sun. Faster and denser wind means more energy hitting Earth's magnetic field.
  • IMF Bz — the direction of the interplanetary magnetic field. When the arrow points down (southward / negative), Earth's magnetic shield opens up and aurora is more likely. The label below tells you whether conditions are favourable or not.
  • Storm Scale — NOAA's G-scale from G0 (none) to G5 (extreme). G1 or above means a geomagnetic storm is officially in progress. G3+ means aurora may be visible across southern England.
Geomagnetic Activity Timeline

The chart at the bottom shows recent and forecast geomagnetic activity over the past few days. The green line is observed storm strength (Kp, 3-hourly), the cyan line is global activity (Hp30, 30-minute resolution), and the blue dashed line is the NOAA forecast. The orange dashed line is your location's aurora threshold — when activity rises above it, aurora becomes possible from your area.

Setting Your Location

Click the location button in the top navigation bar to change your location. Enter any UK postcode and the dashboard will adjust your Kp threshold, cloud forecast, weather conditions, dark hours, and light pollution estimate to match. Your location is saved in your browser so you only need to set it once.

Data Sources

  • NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center
    National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, USA
    • Planetary Kp index (current and 3-day forecast)
    • Solar wind plasma density and speed
    • Interplanetary Magnetic Field (IMF Bz component)
    • NOAA G-scale geomagnetic storm alerts
  • AuroraWatch UK
    Lancaster University, UK
    • Real-time UK geomagnetic activity status (green/yellow/amber/red)
    • UK-specific aurora alert level
  • Open-Meteo
    UK Met Office data via UKMO Seamless models
    • Cloud cover forecasts (low, mid, high cloud layers)
    • UKMO 2km high-resolution model for UK locations
    • Hourly weather data for observing conditions
  • Postcodes.io
    Open-source UK postcode and geolocation API
    • UK postcode geocoding (latitude and longitude)
    • Rural-Urban Classification (RUC11) for light pollution estimation
  • PyEphem
    Astronomical ephemeris library for Python
    • Moon phase calculations and illumination percentage
    • Astronomical, nautical and civil twilight times
    • Sunrise and sunset times for UK locations
  • GFZ Helmholtz Centre for Geosciences
    GFZ German Research Centre for Geosciences, Potsdam
    • Hp30 half-hourly planetary geomagnetic index
    • 30-minute resolution (6× finer than the 3-hourly Kp)
    • Open-ended scale — captures extreme storm peaks above Kp 9
