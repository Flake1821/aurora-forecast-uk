About UK Aurora & Night Sky

UK Aurora & Night Sky provides night-sky alerts for the whole of the UK, covering aurora activity, meteor showers, eclipses, supermoons, planetary events and rare atmospheric phenomena, alongside occasional astrophotography images and tips.

We monitor space weather data from NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center, GFZ Potsdam's half-hourly Hp30 index, and AuroraWatch UK to bring you timely alerts when there's a chance of seeing the aurora borealis or other night sky events from anywhere in the UK.

Aurora visibility depends on your latitude. Southern England (around 50°N) typically needs Kp 5+, while Scotland (around 56°N) may see aurora at Kp 3. Set your location for personalised Kp thresholds and local weather.

How to Read the Dashboard

The dashboard is designed to be understood at a glance, with no scientific jargon. All labels and descriptions use plain English so you can quickly decide whether it's worth heading outside.

  • Gauges are colour-coded from green (quiet) through yellow and orange to red (storm). The text below each gauge tells you what the current reading means.
  • "Should I go outside?" combines aurora activity, cloud cover, moonlight, darkness, and magnetic conditions into a single recommendation. The top two factors driving the verdict are shown as bullet points.
  • IMF Bz shows whether Earth's magnetic shield is open or closed. When the arrow points down (southward), conditions are more favourable for aurora.

Data Sources

  • NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center
    National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, USA
    • Planetary Kp index (current and 3-day forecast)
    • Solar wind plasma density and speed
    • Interplanetary Magnetic Field (IMF Bz component)
    • NOAA G-scale geomagnetic storm alerts
  • AuroraWatch UK
    Lancaster University, UK
    • Real-time UK geomagnetic activity status (green/yellow/amber/red)
    • UK-specific aurora alert level
  • Open-Meteo
    UK Met Office data via UKMO Seamless models
    • Cloud cover forecasts (low, mid, high cloud layers)
    • UKMO 2km high-resolution model for UK locations
    • Hourly weather data for observing conditions
  • Postcodes.io
    Open-source UK postcode and geolocation API
    • UK postcode geocoding (latitude and longitude)
    • Rural-Urban Classification (RUC11) for light pollution estimation
  • PyEphem
    Astronomical ephemeris library for Python
    • Moon phase calculations and illumination percentage
    • Astronomical, nautical and civil twilight times
    • Sunrise and sunset times for UK locations
  • GFZ Helmholtz Centre for Geosciences
    GFZ German Research Centre for Geosciences, Potsdam
    • Hp30 half-hourly planetary geomagnetic index
    • 30-minute resolution (6× finer than the 3-hourly Kp)
    • Open-ended scale — captures extreme storm peaks above Kp 9
